Introducing the Big Mac Burger

The McDonald's Big Mac is a signature burger made with two all-beef patties, special sauce, lettuce, cheese, pickles, and onions, served in a three-part sesame seed bun. It's known for its distinctive taste and iconic layered structure.

The Big Mac is one of McDonald’s most famous menu items, recognized worldwide. It features a unique combination of flavors, highlighted by its tangy "special sauce" and extra middle bun layer, giving it a hearty, satisfying bite.

Ingredients

  • Two all-beef patties
  • Special sauce
  • Shredded lettuce
  • Slices of American cheese
  • Pickles
  • Onions
  • Sesame seed bun

Steps

  1. Toast the buns – Lightly toast the top, bottom, and middle (club) sesame seed buns.
  2. Cook the beef patties – Grill two thin all-beef patties until fully cooked.
  3. Spread the special sauce – Add McDonald’s-style special sauce to the bottom and middle buns.
  4. Add toppings to bottom bun – Place shredded lettuce, chopped onions, and pickles on the bottom bun.
  5. Place first beef patty – Add one beef patty on top of the bottom bun toppings.
  6. Prepare the middle bun – Add shredded lettuce, chopped onions, and a slice of cheese on the middle bun.
  7. Place second beef patty – Add the second patty on top of the middle bun toppings.
  8. Assemble the burger – Stack the middle bun (with toppings and patty) on top of the bottom bun layer, then top it all with the top bun.
